# Vision

## Project Vision

ChickenBehaviorLab aims to become a comprehensive open research framework for skeleton-based poultry behavior analysis, enabling researchers and industry professionals to transform raw farm videos into interpretable behavioral intelligence.

The project is designed to bridge the gap between computer vision, animal behavior analysis, artificial intelligence, and precision livestock farming by providing standardized datasets, annotation protocols, modular software components, and reproducible research methodologies.

Rather than focusing solely on behavior classification, ChickenBehaviorLab seeks to establish a complete ecosystem for understanding poultry behavior across individual, group, and flock levels.

---

# Motivation

Modern poultry farms continuously generate large volumes of video data.

However, extracting meaningful behavioral information from these recordings remains a significant challenge due to the lack of standardized datasets, annotation protocols, behavior ontologies, and modular analysis frameworks.

Most existing research addresses isolated problems such as detection, tracking, or behavior recognition, making it difficult to compare methods or build interoperable systems.

ChickenBehaviorLab was created to address these limitations through a unified research framework.

---

# Mission

Our mission is to develop an open, extensible, and scientifically rigorous framework that supports every stage of automated chicken behavior analysis.

The framework integrates computer vision, pose estimation, graph neural networks, temporal modeling, knowledge representation, and explainable artificial intelligence into a coherent research platform.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The long-term vision of ChickenBehaviorLab is to establish an internationally recognized benchmark framework for poultry behavior analysis.

The project aspires to become a reference platform for:

* Skeleton-based behavior recognition
* Precision livestock farming
* Animal welfare monitoring
* Explainable AI for animal behavior
* Knowledge-driven behavioral analysis
* Early disease and health monitoring
* Mortality risk prediction
* Open scientific collaboration
